# Sprout UX Philosophy: The Feel Constitution

This is not a feature spec. Every other document says what to build; this one protects how it feels. When a decision is fast, cheap, or reasonable but would violate a law below, the law wins.

## One Sentence

Sprout is a calm friend who read your money while you slept and has one small thing to tell you.

Not a dashboard. Not a bank. A character with news. If any screen stops feeling like that, it is wrong, even if it is useful.

## The Seven Laws

### 1. One Hero, One Sentence, One Action

Per screen, per day.

If a screen has two things fighting to be most important, it has zero. Today resolves to total wealth, Sprout's one line, and one button. Sprout carries the emotion; the wealth figure carries the answer. The moment a second co-equal CTA appears, delete one.

Simplicity is not taste here. It is why the check-in takes 30 seconds instead of becoming a chore.

### 2. The Character Carries The Emotion

The UI stays quiet.

Playfulness lives in Sprout: its face, bounce, and voice. It should not come from loud chrome, gradients everywhere, or five accent colors shouting at once.

A calm layout with one expressive character feels delightful. A busy layout with a mascot in the corner feels like another finance app.

The mascot is the warmth. The wealth figure is the user's reason for opening Today. The interface is the calm they sit in. This contrast is the brand.

### 3. Motion Is A Reward

Motion is not decoration.

Animate only to mark change or completion: score count-up, ring sweep, confetti, XP, streak tick, mascot celebration. Never animate to look busy.

Every motion must answer:

- What just happened?
- What changed?
- What did the user complete?

If an animation does not carry meaning, cut it. Cut it first on any phone that drops a frame. A still Sprout at 60fps beats a beautiful Sprout that stutters.

### 4. The Peak Comes Last

Celebration is the end of the loop, never the landing state.

The user must arrive somewhere small and undone, do one thing, and then get the confetti. Land them on "done" and the payoff is spent before they earned it.

The feeling Sprout engineers is closure: "you're okay, you're done." That feeling only works if it comes last.

### 5. Playful On The Way Up, Calm On The Way Down

Never cruel, ever.

Wins get loud: confetti, cheer, XP, streak tick, mascot celebration.

Problems get quiet: watchful Sprout, honest kind sentence, one doable step.

No red-number panic. No frowning mascot. No shame. No guilt. No stern nudges. No streak loss because money was hard.

This asymmetry is the trust model: celebrate progress loudly, deliver bad news gently.

### 6. Glance Is The Product

Depth is the reward for curiosity.

Everything essential should be knowable without studying the screen. Sprout's face is the headline before any number or chart.

Detail is always one tap away and never forced. If a user has to study a chart to feel okay, the screen failed. Never make the user parse what Sprout could say in one line.

### 7. The Empty-Handed User Is The Real User

Design every screen for the person who connected nothing. Auto-capture is a gift on top.

The instant a screen is boring, blank, or blocked until a connection exists, it is broken. In this market, the manual-only user is not an edge case. A gated app is a deleted app.

"Alive with nothing" is not a fallback state. It is the default state to design first.

## Distinctiveness Test

Other finance apps lead with numbers and make people feel behind. Sprout leads with a face and makes people feel okay.

Before shipping any screen, ask:

> If the money values were stripped off, would this screen still have a heartbeat?

If the answer is no, add Sprout's voice until the answer is yes. The money is the content; the character is the product.

Eye-catching does not mean loud. It means one warm, living thing in a calm space. Restraint everywhere else is what makes Sprout pop. Do not add a second bright element to balance it. Let Sprout be the one thing.

## The Three Feel Killers

### 1. Feature Creep On Today

Every quarter someone will want to add "just one card." Today is full. New things go behind a tap or into Money, not onto the hero screen.

### 2. Losing The Performance Trade

Jank creeps in one animation at a time. Test on low-end hardware every release. Cut motion before shipping stutter.

### 3. Bad-Day Cruelty For "Clarity"

A red score or stern nudge may look honest, but it creates the anxiety Sprout exists to remove. Honesty is the calm sentence, not the alarm.

## Final Gut Check

Open the app. Before reading one number, do you feel calmer or more anxious?

If calmer, ship it.

If anxious, the screen fails no matter how correct, feature-complete, or clever it is. That first half-second feeling is the product. Everything else exists to produce it.
